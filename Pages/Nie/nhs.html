Nie Huaisang

"'. . . I don't know, I really don't know!'
This was the only thing Nie Huaisang knew to say when he was desperate.”

Nie Huaisang, Headshaker

Nie Huaisang attended the Gusu Lan lectures the same time Wei Wuxian did. Unlike them, he is gonna attend the lectures again. During the war, he pretended to be sick to not become eligible to go to the Wen Indoctrination camps. He does not excel in martial combat like his brother and his interests lay in the arts instead. Post-Sunshot campaign, despite his brother making him train with his sabre, he is still inclined to the arts, often using Jin Guangyao as a buffer for when his brother urges him to train. He often spent his time painting fans or with his pet birds.

Upon Nie Mingjue's death from qi deviation, Nie Huaisang was forced to take on the mantle of sect leader. He eventually built up a reputation of being incompetent. He gained the namesake of "Headshaker" for when someone asks him one question, his answer is three 'I don't know's. He often asked for help from Nie Mingjue's Sworn Brothers - Lan Xichen and Jin Guangyao -

Wei Wuxian and Lan Wangji encounter him when they came upon the Nie Ancestral Hall and they ask him questions regarding the case about the dismembered fierce corpse. In Koi Tower, he is once again present when Wei Wuxian's identity is exposed as well as when the Burial Mounds was being sieged. Jin Guangyao's crimes were exposed to the cultivation world by someone who brought witnesses.

Eventually, in Guanyin Temple, when Jin Guangyao was disarmed and asked not to move, when things seemed to have ended, Nie Huaisang made his move. He screamed that Jin Guangyao was gonna attack, which tricked Lan Xichen into stabbing him, ultimately killing him. At the end of these events, only Wei Wuxian deduced who orchestrated his resurrection to expose Jin Guangyao and even he has no way to prove it. The one who gave Mo Xuanyu the sacrifice ritual, the one who made them solve the case of the dismembered fierce corpse, the one who brought the witnesses to Jin Guangyao's crimes, is Nie Huaisang, the Headshaker.

Huaisang's Name
Huaisang's Name

In Chinese culture, names often reflect the parent's desires or expectations for their child. The character 桑 sāng means mulberries. Mulberry leaves are grown and fed to silkworms to make silk of the highest quality. Farming is the work of the common people, but growing mulberries instead of essential goods like cotton suggest the wealth and security needed to indulge. Thus, it appears that Nie Huaisang's parents expected him to be someone who prizes simple luxuries and lives a comfortable, unambitious life.
