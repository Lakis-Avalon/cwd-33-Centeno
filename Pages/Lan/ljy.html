Lan Jingyi

Lan Jingyi is a lively and outspoken junior disciple of the Gusu Lan Clan whose sharp tongue and candid personality often contrast with the typically composed and disciplined demeanor of his peers. Throughout Mo Dao Zu Shi, he proves himself to be both brave and kindhearted, though his impulsiveness and quick temper occasionally lead to amusing or tense situations. Jingyi plays a prominent role in several key events, showcasing his unwavering loyalty to his friends, especially Lan Sizhui, and his occasional begrudging respect for Wei Wuxian.

In Mo Village, Jingyi is the first to address the newly resurrected Wei Wuxian, albeit with irritation after Wuxian's antics. His bravery and leadership shine when he uses a signal to alert Lan Wangji during the crisis caused by the Demonic Left Arm. Despite his loud demeanor, Jingyi demonstrates a strong sense of responsibility, attempting to keep the frightened servants calm amidst the chaos. Later, he accompanies the Gusu Lan juniors to Dafan Mountain, where his loyalty to Lan Wangji becomes evident as he fiercely defends him against Jiang Cheng's accusations.

Jingyi's interactions with Wei Wuxian often reveal his fiery personality. Back at the Cloud Recesses, he becomes indignant at Wuxian's teasing remarks and is quick to stand up for Lan Wangji and the clan's honor. However, his bravery and curiosity lead him to assist in dangerous investigations, such as in Yi City, where he contracts corpse poisoning.

Lan Jingyi's comedic yet heartfelt moments add levity to tense situations, such as his incredulous reaction upon seeing Lan Wangji tie his forehead ribbon around Wei Wuxian's wrist. In moments of crisis, like the Second Siege of the Burial Mounds, Jingyi stands alongside his friends, courageously defending Wei Wuxian and insisting on fighting despite overwhelming odds. His loyalty extends even to Wen Ning, as he encourages the juniors to accept and support the ghost general despite his fearsome reputation.
