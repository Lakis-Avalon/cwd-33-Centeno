Wen Ruohan

"Having an exceedingly high cultivation base, Wen Ruohan is able to preserve his physical body in peak condition and his voice is that of a young man"

Wen Ruohan

Wen Ruohan, the tyrannical leader of the Qishan Wen Clan, was a cultivator of exceptional talent and unparalleled ambition. He sought to dominate the cultivation world through fear, annexing smaller sects and forcing larger ones into submission. Under his reign, the Wen Clan launched devastating attacks, including the burning of the Cloud Recesses and the Lotus Pier Massacre, actions that cemented his reputation as a ruthless conqueror.

Wen Ruohan was responsible for the death of the Nie Clan's leader, father of Nie Mingjue and Nie Huaisang, further destabilizing the Nie Sect and extending his influence. Known for his cruelty, he maintained the infamous Inferno Palace, a place of unspeakable torture where he crushed dissenters and punished enemies.

However, his ambition ultimately led to his downfall when his tyranny provoked a rebellion. The Sunshot Campaign, led by the remaining great sects, was initiated to end his reign of terror. Despite his immense power and strategic cunning, the war began to turn against the Wen Clan.

A significant blow came when Nie Mingjue killed Wen Xu during a key battle in the campaign. Wen Ruohan was enraged by his son's death but faced mounting challenges. Wei Wuxian, using his cultivation techniques, emerged as a significant threat to the Wens, tipping the scales further against them.

In the final stages of the war, Wen Ruohan was personally confronted by Lan Xichen and Nie Mingjue, demonstrating his extraordinary skill by fighting both of them with ease. However, his overconfidence proved fatal. He was betrayed by Meng Yao, his trusted subordinate, who struck him down at the most critical moment. This act of treachery led to his defeat and death, bringing an end to his reign of terror and dismantling the Wen Clan's dominance.

In the live action, they made Wen Ruohan do demonic cultivation, however in the novel he is not. He is just a ridiculously strong and talented cultivator who cultivates the same paths as the others. He used it of course for his tyranny.

People made him a chief cultivator in the live action. This is far from the novel since the sects were quite independent from each other at the time and had no 'higher up' person they report to. There was no chief cultivator in the novel. The Wens were just THAT strong.
