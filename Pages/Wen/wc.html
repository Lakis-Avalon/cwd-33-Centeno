Wen Chao

"'All those who oppress others and do evil relying on the power of their house should be killed. Not only that, they should be beheaded for tens of thousands to revile so that those to come would beware.'

'For insulting the Wen Clan, you can die now, Wen Chao' "

Wen Chao

Wen Chao, the second son of Wen Ruohan, epitomized arrogance and cowardice, wielding his family's power with reckless abandon. Shielded by the Wen Clan's dominance and the unwavering protection of Wen Zhuliu, the "Core-Melting Hand," Wen Chao carried himself with an air of invincibility while rarely facing danger himself. He relied heavily on his status and his guardian's strength, which emboldened his cruelty and contempt for others.

During the hunt for the Xuanwu of Slaughter, Wen Chao revealed both his depravity and cowardice. He attempted to assault Luo Qingyang (later known as Mianmian), exploiting his position to satisfy his lascivious desires. His failure to succeed in this vile act highlighted his dependency on others to enforce his will, as his threats were more bark than bite when unsupported. He also clearly did not know the Wen doctrine as when Wei Wuxian quoted the Wen's teachings, he thought it was from the Lans, insulting his clan in the process. The supposed punishment was death, but of course, it only led to his embarrassment. He also left the sect heirs to die when Wei Wuxian, Lan Wangji, and Jin Zixuan stood up to him trying to use humans as live bait leading to a commotion that woke up the Xuanwu of Slaughter.

He is present when Lotus Pier was massacred. He eventually caught Wei Wuxian who was weakened from his surgery. After Wen Zhuliu uses his technique on Wei Wuxian, they throw him into the Burial Mounds leaving him to die. Unfortunately for him, Wei Wuxian came back even stronger and he hunted Wen Chao, giving him a slow agonizing death as he chased him for weeks.
