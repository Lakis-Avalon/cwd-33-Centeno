MOLING SU SECT LEADER

Su She

"It's one thing for you to slander me, but to slander Lianfang-zun..."

Su She

Originally from the Gusu Lan Sect, Su She struggled to meet the high standards of the sect, especially when compared to Lan Wangji, whom he deeply admired but could never match. Su She emulated Lan Wangji by adopting his habits, such as playing the guqin and fighting with a sword, but his attempts only led to ridicule as others viewed him as a poor imitation. His resentment festered over time, and he eventually left the Lan Sect to form his own, the Moling Su Sect.

Su She later became a staunch ally of Jin Guangyao, finding validation in the latter's acknowledgment of him. Under Jin Guangyao's influence, Su She became involved in numerous schemes. Notably, he was responsible for casting the Hundred Holes Curse on Jin Zixun, but the blame was shifted onto Wei Wuxian, adding to the false accusations that tarnished Wei Wuxian's reputation. Su She and the Moling Su Sect essentially became lackeys for Jin Guangyao, aiding him in his manipulations and consolidating his power.

After Wei Wuxian's resurrection, Su She played a key role in the second Burial Mounds siege. He used his guqin to perform a song that disabled the spiritual energy of the cultivators present so that if they die in the burial mounds, Wei Wuxian would be blamed. However, their schemes unraveled as evidence of their crimes came to light. Su She was eventually exposed as the true perpetrator of the Hundred Holes Curse on Jin Zixun, further discrediting him.

In the final confrontation at Guanyin Temple, Su She's loyalty to Jin Guangyao remained steadfast to the end. When Nie Mingjue's fierce corpse attacked Jin Guangyao, Su She attempted to protect him but was ultimately killed in the process.
