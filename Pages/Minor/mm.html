ROGUE CULTIVATOR

Luo Qingyang

"If the Jin Cultivators truly abused the Wen Remnants in the camps, then Wei Wuxian didn't commit murder, it was revenge"

Luo Qingyang

Luo Qingyang, often referred to by her nickname Mianmian, is a courageous and principled cultivator whose life intersects with Wei Wuxian, Lan Wangji, and the rest of the younger generation during critical moments in the story.
She first appears during the Wen Clan indoctrination camp alongside other young cultivators like Wei Wuxian and Lan Wangji. Wei Wuxian once flirted with her, asking for herbs from her pouch to help Lan Wangji recover from injuries, an act that upset Lan Wangji, who chastised Wei Wuxian for playing around. During the same camp, Wen Chao attempted to assault her, which his mistress Wang Lingjiao clocked as a threat to her position. Later, Wang Lingjiao maliciously volunteered Mianmian to be live bait to lure out the Xuanwu of Slaughter. Lan Wangji and Jin Zixuan stood up for her, and while Wei Wuxian also tried to intervene, Jiang Wanyin stopped him to avoid them getting in trouble.

In the chaos, Wei Wuxian held Wen Chao hostage to temporarily diffuse the situation, but the beast attacked, forcing everyone to flee. The situation escalated when Wen Chao trapped the sect heirs in the Xuanwu's habitat after the beast was awakened. While Lan Wangji and Wei Wuxian were trapped in the cave with the Xuanwu, Wei Wuxian mistakenly believed Lan Wangji was upset because he liked Mianmian, which led to a humorous misunderstanding between the two. Notably, the herbs Mianmian gave Wei Wuxian in her pouch were later used to treat their injuries. Lan Wangji kept the pouch for 13 years, a poignant memento during Wei Wuxian's absence.

Mianmian reappears in a flashback during the heated debates about Wei Wuxian's actions post-Sunshot Campaign. While others accused Wei Wuxian of abusing his power by killing the Jins, Mianmian bravely defended him, pointing out that the Jins had killed the Wen remnants who had aided Wei Wuxian, making his actions justifiable revenge. Her defense was dismissed due to her gender and the fact that Wei Wuxian had saved her life in the past, but her moral courage shone through. Disillusioned by the corruption and hypocrisy of the cultivation world, she left her sect and became a rogue cultivator. Nie Mingjue himself praised Mianmian, acknowledging that she had more backbone than many others in the cultivation world. In the post-canon timeline, Mianmian is happily married and travels as a cultivator with her husband and their daughter, whom they also named Mianmian, showing that she found peace and happiness despite the chaos she once endured.

Trivias

Her Name
Sheep

The 青 character in Luo Qingyang's name, while usually translated as 'green' in modern Chinese, was usually translated as 'black' in classical Chinese. Thus, her name is likely meant as "black sheep".

Affiliation
Minor to Rogue

In the live action, they made Mianmian a part of the Jin Sect, however in the novel, she is part of a minor sect, the Golden Point Hall.
